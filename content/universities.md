sections:
  - heading: "The Challenge of Campus Navigation"
    paragraphs:
      - "University campuses are intricate mazes of lecture halls, libraries, student centers, and dormitories, reflecting centuries of history and modern development alike. This information is typically represented on one or more maps on the institution's website. For students and staff with disabilities, especially blind and low vision students, learning these terrains can be daunting. Traditional digital maps are only visual. This means they are completely blank to totally blind screen reader users. Traditional maps are also difficult for low vision users to operate due to their low contrast, inability to zoom to 200%, and other issues faced by low vision users. The traditional maps force visual usage, which is very exclusionary, even to people who have no visual disability, but prefer to use a non-visual experience."
